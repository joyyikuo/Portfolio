MBTI forum post analysis with gensim tf-idf¶

While I was looking for potential toy datasets to begin a product feature development for my current OPTiMAL staffing product at TheAIWorks, I ran into this interesting MBTI forum post dataset on Kaggle that gathered over 8600 rows of users with 50 most recent posts per user. One aspect of personality I was always interested in was if personality was "observable" through text.

The Kaggle dataset can be found here.

Despite this dataset being slightly biased to begin with due to the source being a personality forum where people are more likely to talk about their personality than other forms of text (Twitter, Facebook, Slack, email...etc.), it is still an interesting case study to see how different personalities communicate.

First, we take a look at how the dataset is laid out. For convenience, we'll be labelling the types by:

Introversion (I) vs. Extraversion (E), Sensing (S) vs. Intuition (N), Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F), and Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P).

This will smooth the process of sorting the personality types with boolean labels.

from gensim.parsing.preprocessing import strip_tags, strip_punctuation, strip_numeric, strip_multiple_whitespaces
from gensim.parsing.preprocessing import remove_stopwords, preprocess_string, strip_short

CUSTOM_FILTERS = [lambda x: x.lower(), strip_tags, strip_punctuation, strip_numeric, strip_multiple_whitespaces,
                 remove_stopwords, strip_short]

ind_word = []
for posts in ind_post:
    current_ind = []
    for post in posts:
        words_in_post = preprocess_string(post, CUSTOM_FILTERS)
        current_ind += words_in_post
    ind_word.append(current_ind)

Next, we begin our task of computing tf-idf values with the ease provided to us via the gensim packages. By building a dictionary with all individual words in our corpus and modelling each document by each user, we obtain a tf-idf model for us to work with.

PART 3: Categorised Analysis¶

In order to start analysing our computed values by personality grouping, we will need to compute group-wise tf-idf values. This could be accomplished through summing tf-idf values on the document level, as simply by sorting all tf-idf values will result in emphasis on 'individual topics', not 'group topics'.

For this case study, we will only be looking at Extraversion vs. Introversion, as completing this analysis on all personality types would be rather repetitive (not that it would not be interesting though!).

We will be sorting the summed tf-idf values from the model into a nice DataFrame for us to visualise, with extraversion words and introversion words each filling one DataFrame:

We can visualise the information above with a nice bar graph by tf-idf values, using the word frequency as the color for enriching the visualisation with information. We can notice a couple of things from our first visualisation attempt:

  1. Introverted posts contain more links like jpg or youtube links compared to extroverted posts
  2. Both introverted and extroverted posts contain a lot of reference to their personality types, due to the data source being from a personality forum
  3. The introverted group has a higher tf-idf value sum compared to the extroverted group, which may be due to a dataset imbalance (more introverted users than extroverted ones); we will need to verify this later
  4. We can spot some overlapping common words that are used by both groups such as 'lol' and 'welcome', as they are common words in any forum under any context, but we can also spot group-unique words such as 'music'. This does not indicate that extroverts never talk about music on a personality forum, just that they might do it less compared to introverted individuals.
  5. Words like 'http' and 'com' act like stop words in our case, as they do not hold additional information other than that the users posted a link. As the links are segmented in our case, they hold very little value in our analysis

Next, we try to address the issues we encountered during our first visualisation attempt:

  • abundance of personality profile references
  • additional stop words such as 'http' and 'com'

We will need a different approach for the two issues, as personality profile references sometimes appear in different forms such as plurals 'entps' for 'entp'. This means partial match for these personality profiles will need to be included for removal. In contrast, 'com' needs exact matching, as partial-matching 'com' could easily take out words such as 'compassion' or 'compass', which are important words with context in our analysis.

And the perfect solution seems to be a word cloud venn diagram! By using the handy wordcloud venn package provided by paulbrodersen (try it out here!), We could easily see the unique words for each group in the top 100 words used by introverts and extroverts on the personality forum.

While most words are used by both groups, some unique words seem quite fitting given the personality type, such as:

  • 'problem' and 'feeling' for introverts, as introverts are more reflective and think before they do
  • more "exciting" words such as 'crazy' and 'awesome' for extroverts, as these words are relatively attention grabbing
  • 'music' for introverts, as enjoying music is more often an individual activity than a group activity
  • 'sex' for extroverts, as extroverts might feel more comfortable sharing a private activity with strangers online than introverts who are less outgoing

While these observations are speculations based on words observed in each personality group based on tf-idf alone, and subjected to the limitation of the dataset and data source, it is still an interesting case study to showcase what insights and information simple text processing can provide us with.
